In this tutorial, we’ll learn how to search for specimen and sample records in the NEON Biorepository Sample Portal and interpret the information associated with archived materials. We’ll explore the search form, search results, and individual occurrence records, along with related resources that connect archived materials to associated datasets and workflows.

<div id="ds-objectives" markdown="1">

## Learning objectives

After completing this tutorial, you will be able to:

* Search for archived materials in the NEON Biorepository  
* Use filters to refine specimen and sample searches  
* Interpret information provided in occurrence records  
* Navigate relationships among archived materials and associated datasets  
* Export and download specimen and sample records

## Understanding Individual Occurrence Records

Selecting a result opens an individual occurrence record page containing detailed metadata about the archived material.

Occurrence records may include:

* Scientific identification information  
* Taxonomic hierarchy  
* Collection date and locality  
* NEON site information  
* Sampling event metadata  
* Preservation and preparation methods  
* Collector and identifier information  
* Associated images and media  
* Genetic references and linked resources  
* Relationships to associated archived materials  

Because many NEON workflows generate multiple archived materials from the same parent sample or collection event, occurrence records may also link to associated tissues, extracts, filters, vouchers, or related materials.

## Linked Resources and Associated Data

Occurrence records often connect to additional NEON resources.

Depending on the sample type, records may link to:

* Sample Type Profiles  
* Related NEON Data Products  
* Protocol documentation  
* Genetic resources  
* GBIF occurrence records  
* Images and media  

These linked resources provide additional context about how samples were collected, processed, analyzed, and related to associated datasets.
