This tutorial series introduces users to the NEON Biorepository and walks through the core tasks involved in working with specimens: understanding what is available, finding relevant samples, requesting materials, properly attributing their use and working with downloaded data.

The NEON Biorepository provides access to a wide range of interconnected specimen and sample types collected across NEON field sites. These tutorials are designed to help users efficiently navigate the system and make effective use of Biorepository resources.

## Series Overview

1. Understanding NEON Sample Types  
2. Searching for and Interpreting Records - _in progress_
3. Requesting Sample Loans - _in progress_
4. Attributing and Sharing Sample Use - _in progress_
5. Working with Downloaded Sample Data - _in progress_

---

## Tutorials in This Series

### 1. Understanding NEON Sample Types

Learn about the types of samples and specimens archived in the NEON Biorepository.

Topics covered:
- Types of materials archived in the Biorepository  
- Sample Type Browser  
- Sample Type Profiles
- Identified Sample Types
- Finding connections among sample types, protocols, and associated data products 

---

### 2. Searching for and Interpreting Records - _in progress_

Learn how to use the NEON Biorepository Sample Portal to locate and understand samples.

Topics covered:
- Selecting sample types  
- Applying filters (location, date, taxonomy, properties)  
- Navigating search results  
- Understanding specimen record fields (taxonomy, identifiers, collection data)  
- Recognizing relationships between sample types (e.g., specimen ↔ tissue ↔ DNA extract)  
- Interpreting what a record represents  

---

### 3. Requesting Sample Loans - _in progress_

Learn how to request physical samples from the NEON Biorepository and what to expect during the process.

Topics covered:
- Submitting a loan request  
- Overview of the review process  
- Understanding the Sample Use Agreement and sample use policies  
- What to expect for preparation, shipping, and delivery  

---

### 4. Attributing and Sharing Sample Use - _in progress_

Learn how to properly acknowledge and share your use of NEON Biorepository samples.

Topics covered:
- Generating citations for specimens and datasets  
- Using the provided citation tools and links  
- Making your sample use publicly visible through NEON  
- Best practices for attribution in publications and products  

---

### 5. Working with Downloaded Sample Data - _in progress_

Learn how to use specimen data downloaded from the portal.

Topics covered:
- Downloading search results  
- Understanding Darwin Core (DwC) fields  
- Darwin Core Archive (DwC-A) and star schema structure  
- Integrating Biorepository data with other NEON datasets
